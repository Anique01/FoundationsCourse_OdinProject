Ratatouille

Description

A classic French vegetable stew that’s light yet flavorful, packed with vibrant Mediterranean vegetables.

Ratatouille is a classic French dish that celebrates the vibrant flavors of summer vegetables. Originating from Provence, this hearty vegetable stew combines eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, tomatoes, onions, and garlic, all simmered together with fragrant herbs like thyme and basil. The vegetables are cooked until tender, absorbing the rich flavors of olive oil and herbs. Ratatouille is both light and satisfying, offering a colorful and nutritious way to enjoy seasonal produce. It can be served as a main dish with crusty bread or as a side to complement meats or fish, making it a versatile addition to any meal.

ingredients

  • 2 aubergines, sliced
  • 2 courgettes, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until softened.
  2. Add the bell peppers, aubergine, and courgette. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until they begin to soften.
  3. Add the tomatoes, thyme, basil, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together and let it cook on low heat for about 20-30 minutes, until the vegetables are tender and the flavors meld.
  4. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve with crusty bread or over rice.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Ratatouille!
