Enchiladas

Description

A comforting and flavorful Mexican classic with tender chicken wrapped in corn tortillas, covered in a rich red sauce, and topped with melted cheese.

Enchiladas are a beloved Mexican dish that features soft corn tortillas filled with a variety of ingredients, typically shredded meat, cheese, or beans, and then rolled up and smothered in a rich, flavorful sauce. The sauce, often made from red chilies, tomatoes, and spices, gives the enchiladas their signature bold and slightly spicy flavor. After baking, the tortillas become tender while the sauce infuses each bite with warmth and depth. Topped with melted cheese, sour cream, and fresh cilantro, enchiladas are a comforting and satisfying dish perfect for any occasion, offering a delicious combination of textures and tastes in every bite.

ingredients

  • 500g chicken breast, cooked and shredded
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 400g red enchilada sauce
  • 200g grated cheese (cheddar or a Mexican blend)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Sour cream, for serving
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the chicken:

    • Cook the chicken breasts in a pot of boiling water or in a skillet with a bit of oil until fully cooked. Once cooled, shred the chicken with two forks and set aside.

  2. Prepare the filling:

    • In a pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté for about 5 minutes until softened.
    • Stir in the shredded chicken, cumin, chili powder, paprika, and salt. Mix well and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to combine the flavors.

  3. Assemble the enchiladas:

    • Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
    • Lightly fry the corn tortillas in a hot pan with a bit of oil for about 30 seconds on each side to soften them. Drain excess oil on paper towels.
    • Dip each tortilla in the enchilada sauce, then fill with the chicken mixture and roll up tightly. Place the rolled tortillas seam-side down in a baking dish.

  4. Bake the enchiladas:

    • Once all the enchiladas are in the baking dish, pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the top and sprinkle with grated cheese.
    • Bake in the oven for about 20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  5. Serve:

    • Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with a side of sour cream.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Enchiladas!
